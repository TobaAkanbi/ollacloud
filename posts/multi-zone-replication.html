How we built multi-zone replication across Nigerian data centres

O
OllaCloud Engineering
· Jun 2026 · 8 min read

The challenge

When you store data in a single availability zone, a hardware failure can mean data loss. Our goal from day one was to make OllaCloud storage durable enough that customers never have to think about it.

Building cross-zone replication inside Nigeria meant working with the physical realities of our data centre footprint — latency between sites, bandwidth costs, and the need to keep everything within Nigerian borders for NDPA compliance.

How replication works

Every write to OllaCloud object storage is synchronously replicated to a secondary zone before we return a success response to your application. This means:

  • No data is ever acknowledged until it exists in at least two zones.
  • A full zone failure causes zero data loss — recovery is automatic.
  • Your application code doesn't change — replication is transparent at the API level.

What this means for you

You get 99.999999999% (11 nines) durability on object storage with no additional configuration. Block volumes on our Growth and Enterprise plans are also replicated by default.

Use olla storage ls --zone to inspect which zones your buckets are replicated across.
