FAQs

Questions, answered.

Every byte you store on OllaCloud lives on OCI-compatible rack-scale infrastructure located inside Nigeria. Your data does not leave the country unless you explicitly configure it to. This keeps you aligned with the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA), simplifies regulatory conversations, and means zero data-sovereignty risk from foreign government data requests.

OllaCloud is built on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI)-compatible rack-scale hardware, the same architecture used by global hyperscalers. This means you get enterprise-grade compute (552–2208 OCPUs, 6–26 TB RAM), S3-compatible object storage, and upstream-compatible Kubernetes, all from a platform that lives in Lagos. Your existing OCI or AWS tooling, SDKs, and Terraform modules work without modification.

The infrastructure primitives are equivalent: compute, block and object storage, virtual networking, managed Kubernetes but with three key advantages: single-digit-millisecond latency to Nigerian users (versus 80–150 ms from European regions), billing in Naira with no forex exposure, and full data residency within Nigerian borders. You also get a 3-layer inbuilt firewall and compute enclave isolation that is architecturally on par with the global tier-1 providers.

OllaCloud operates on a 99.99% SLA backed by redundant rack-scale infrastructure, multi-zone replication and automated failover. If we fall short of the committed availability level, service credits apply per the SLA terms. Our in-country engineering and security operations team monitors the platform 24/7.

OllaCloud enforces a 3-layer inbuilt firewall and compute enclave isolation, meaning each tenant's workload is separated at the hardware level not just by software policy. All data at rest is encrypted with AES-256. OllaCloud administrators have zero access to your data or your virtual infrastructure. Your Virtual Cloud Network (VCN) is fully private by default.
