What is data residency in Nigeria?

Data residency means your data never leaves Nigeria — not to be stored, not to be processed, not to be backed up.

Data residency is the requirement that data is stored and processed only within a specified geographic boundary — in this case, Nigeria. It is different from data sovereignty, which is about who governs the data. Residency is purely about physical location.

Under the NDPA 2023 and CBN cybersecurity framework, Nigerian organisations — especially banks and fintechs — must ensure that personal and financial data does not leave Nigeria without meeting strict legal conditions. The simplest, most provable way to satisfy this requirement is to run your infrastructure on hardware physically located inside Nigeria.

OllaCloud is deployed on-premises inside your Nigerian facility or a Nigerian co-location data centre. There is no overseas backup region, no data replication to foreign servers, no cross-border transfer risk — by design.

Nigeria's data residency regulatory framework

Three regulators. One clear answer: your data must stay in Nigeria.

NDPA, CBN, and NITDA each impose data localisation requirements from different angles. OllaCloud satisfies all three simultaneously.

NDPA 2023

Section 43 restricts transfer of personal data outside Nigeria unless strict conditions are met. Penalties: up to 2% of annual gross revenue or ₦10 million.

CBN Cybersecurity Framework

Requires all customer data, transaction records, and core banking data to be stored and processed within Nigeria. Applies to all CBN-licensed institutions.

NITDA Localisation

National Information Technology Development Agency requires government and regulated sector data to be processed and stored on infrastructure located in Nigeria.

How OllaCloud guarantees data residency

Architecture, not policy. Provable, not assumed.

Most cloud providers address data residency through contractual promises — a clause in a Data Processing Agreement that says your data will stay in a specified region. Contractual promises are not the same as architectural guarantees. A provider can change their infrastructure, apply for a government data request, or replicate data for operational reasons — and your contract will not stop them.

OllaCloud is different. The hardware is inside your facility. It has no connection to overseas infrastructure unless you explicitly configure one. Data residency is not a policy — it is a physical fact.

  • On-premises deployment inside your facility
    The OllaCloud appliance is installed in your Lagos or Abuja data centre. Your data physically cannot leave unless you send it.
  • Zero provider access to your data
    3-layer inbuilt firewall, compute enclave isolation, and AES-256 encryption at rest mean OllaCloud administrators have no access to your virtual infrastructure or data.
  • Audit-ready compliance documentation
    Olla Systems provides architecture diagrams, access logs, and the documentation package needed for NDPC and CBN examinations.
  • Backups stay in Nigeria too
    Cross-zone replication and backup policies are configured within Nigeria — your secondary copy is also Nigeria-resident.
